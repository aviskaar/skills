# Proposal Templates

Three proposal templates: **Internal Business Case**, **Customer Strategic Proposal**, and **Executive/Board Briefing**. Use the `proposal-writer` skill to populate these, or use directly as standalone templates.

---

## Template A — Internal Business Case

Use for: Resource requests, initiative approvals, strategic pivots, cross-functional commitments.

```
# [Initiative Name] — Business Case
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Author**: [PM name]
**Decision Requested**: [Specific ask — headcount, budget, go/no-go, timeline approval]
**Decision Needed By**: [date]
**Approvers**: [names]
**Reviewers**: [names]

---

## TL;DR (Executive Summary)
[3–5 bullet points. Answer: What, Why, How much, Return, Risk if we don't act.]

- **What**: [one sentence on the initiative]
- **Why now**: [one sentence on urgency]
- **Ask**: [specific resource request]
- **Expected return**: [quantified outcome]
- **Risk of inaction**: [what we lose or miss]

---

## The Situation
[What is happening in the market, with customers, or competitively that requires action?
Include: data, customer quotes, competitive signals. Do not pad with context that isn't load-bearing.]

**Key data points**:
- [stat or metric with source]
- [customer quote with attribution]
- [competitive signal with source]

---

## The Opportunity
[What specific opportunity can we capture? How big is it?]

| Opportunity Dimension | Estimate | Source / Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue potential | $[X]k–$[X]M | [source — Confirmed/Estimated] |
| Accounts at risk if we don't act | [X] accounts / $[X]k ARR | [source] |
| Competitive win rate improvement | [X]% → [Y]% | [source] |
| NPS / retention impact | [X] point improvement | [source] |

---

## The Proposal
[What specifically are we asking to build, buy, or change?]

**Scope**: [What is in scope — be explicit]
**Not in scope**: [What this initiative does NOT include]

**High-level approach**:
1. [Phase 1 — what, outcome, timeline]
2. [Phase 2 — what, outcome, timeline]
3. [Phase 3 — what, outcome, timeline]

**Resources required**:
| Resource | Amount | Duration | From |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engineering | [X] engineers | [Y] months | [team] |
| Design | [X] designers | [Y] months | [team] |
| PM | [X] % PM time | [Y] months | [name] |
| Budget | $[X]k | [period] | [budget line] |

---

## Return on Investment

**Success Metrics**:
| Metric | Baseline | 3-Month Target | 12-Month Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| [metric] | [value] | [value] | [value] |

**Financial case** (if applicable):
- Year 1 cost: $[X]k
- Year 1 revenue impact: $[X]k
- Payback period: [X] months
- 3-year NPV: $[X]k

**Counter-metric** (what we'll watch to ensure we're not breaking something):
- [metric and alert threshold]

---

## Alternatives Considered
| Alternative | Why Considered | Why Not Chosen |
|---|---|---|
| [Option A] | [rationale] | [honest reason] |
| [Option B] | [rationale] | [honest reason] |
| Do Nothing | Always a valid alternative | [quantified cost of inaction] |

---

## Risks and Mitigations
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Technical risk] | H/M/L | H/M/L | [plan] |
| [Market risk] | H/M/L | H/M/L | [plan] |
| [Resource risk] | H/M/L | H/M/L | [plan] |

---

## Ask and Next Steps
**Decision requested**: [Exact approval needed]
**If approved**:
1. [Next step — owner — date]
2. [Next step — owner — date]

**If not approved**:
[What alternative path will be pursued — shows the PM has thought this through]

**Questions or concerns**: [name] — [email/Slack]
```

## Template B — Customer Strategic Proposal

Use for: Enterprise account proposals, co-development programs, beta partnerships, strategic expansions.

```
# [Proposal Title] — Prepared for [Customer Name]
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Prepared by**: [Your company] | [PM name] | [email]
**Proposal valid until**: [date]
**Classification**: Confidential — For [Customer Name] only

---

## Opening Statement
[1–2 sentences that demonstrate you understand this customer's specific situation.
DO NOT use: "In today's digital landscape..." or any generic opener.
DO use: Reference something specific about their business, their stated goals, or their industry.]

---

## We Heard You
[Reflect back what you've learned from conversations, discovery, or account research.
Show that you've listened — use their language, reference their stated priorities.]

**Your stated priorities**:
- [Priority 1 — use their words]
- [Priority 2 — use their words]
- [Priority 3 — use their words]

**Challenges you're navigating**:
- [Challenge 1]
- [Challenge 2]

---

## The Opportunity We See Together
[Frame why this is the right moment for this partnership / expansion / program.
Be specific to them — not a generic market narrative.]

[How does their situation intersect with where our product is heading?
What becomes possible for them that wasn't before?]

---

## Our Proposed Approach

### What We're Proposing
[Clear description of what you're offering — product, program, partnership, access.
Outcome-focused, not feature-focused.]

### How It Works
**Phase 1 — [Phase Name]** ([timeframe]):
- [What happens]
- [What customer gets]
- [What we need from them]

**Phase 2 — [Phase Name]** ([timeframe]):
- [What happens]
- [What customer gets]

**Phase 3 — [Phase Name] / Ongoing** ([timeframe]):
- [Steady state / ongoing value]

### What We Commit To
- [Specific deliverable — [date]]
- [Specific deliverable — [date]]
- [Named point of contact and response SLA]

---

## Your Investment
[Honest, clear statement of what you're asking from them.]

| Ask | Detail |
|---|---|
| Time commitment | [e.g., 2 hours/month for feedback sessions] |
| Data / access | [e.g., anonymized usage logs for model training] |
| Co-funding | [if applicable — $X] |
| Internal champion | [a named executive sponsor] |
| Feedback commitment | [e.g., monthly product review with PM] |

---

## Value You'll Receive

### Near-Term (0–90 days)
- [Specific, measurable benefit]
- [Specific, measurable benefit]

### Medium-Term (3–12 months)
- [Specific outcome]
- [Specific outcome]

### Strategic Value
- [How this positions them competitively or strategically]

**Evidence from similar customers** (anonymized):
- "[Customer type] reduced [metric] by [X]% within [timeframe]."
- "[Customer type] achieved [outcome] after [program]."

---

## Why Now
[Factual reasoning for timing — not manufactured urgency.
What is happening in their market, regulatory environment, or competitive landscape that makes this the right moment?]

---

## Next Steps
| Action | Owner | By When |
|---|---|---|
| [e.g., Review this proposal with your team] | [Customer name] | [date] |
| [e.g., Schedule alignment call] | [PM name] | [date] |
| [e.g., Execute NDA for data sharing] | Both | [date] |
| [e.g., Kick off Phase 1] | Both | [date] |

**Your contact**: [PM name] | [email] | [phone]

---

*This proposal reflects our current planning direction. Features described as "in exploration" are not committed roadmap items. All timelines are estimates and subject to change. Specific contractual commitments will be captured in a formal agreement.*
```

## Template C — Executive / Board Strategy Brief

Use for: Board meetings, investor updates, executive team strategy reviews, QBRs with leadership.

```
# [Product Area / Portfolio] — Strategy Brief
**Prepared for**: [Board / Executive Team / Investors]
**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD] | **Presented by**: [CPO / PM name]
**Classification**: Confidential

---

## Situation
[2–3 sentences: What is the current state of the market and our position in it?
Be honest — do not spin. Boards detect spin and it erodes trust.]

**Key metrics** (current):
| Metric | Value | Trend | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Revenue / ARR] | $[X]M | ↑/↓ [X]% QoQ | [comp] |
| [Win rate] | [X]% | ↑/↓ | [comp] |
| [NPS / retention] | [X] | ↑/↓ | [comp] |
| [Market share estimate] | [X]% | ↑/↓ | [comp] |

---

## What We're Seeing
[The 3 most important things we've learned from market, customers, and competitors in the last quarter.
Prioritize signal over noise. Boards want insight, not status updates.]

1. **[Insight 1]**: [What we observed] → [What it means for strategy]
2. **[Insight 2]**: [What we observed] → [What it means for strategy]
3. **[Insight 3]**: [What we observed] → [What it means for strategy]

---

## Our Strategic Response
[What decisions we've made or are recommending based on these insights.
Each decision should link to an insight above.]

| Decision | Rationale | OKR Impact | Resources Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| [e.g., Accelerate Platform X investment] | [link to insight] | [OKR Y] | [$X, X engineers] |
| [e.g., Enter [vertical] market in H2] | [link to insight] | [OKR Z] | [$X, X months] |

---

## Portfolio Performance vs. Plan
| Product Area | Q[X] Plan | Q[X] Actual | Commentary |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Area 1] | [target] | [actual] | [one sentence — no spin] |

---

## Strategic Bets and Stage Gates
| Bet | Investment to Date | Next Milestone | Status | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [H3 bet name] | $[X]k | [milestone] | On track / At risk | Continue / Pivot / Stop |

---

## Risks on the Radar
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Our Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Competitor X launches Y] | Medium | High | [Planned response] |
| [Regulatory change Z] | Low | High | [Monitoring; prepared response] |

---

## Ask from This Group
[Be explicit about what you need from the board or executive team.
One ask per bullet. No vague asks.]

- **Decision**: [Specific approval needed — Y/N, by [date]]
- **Guidance**: [Area where you want strategic input]
- **Resources**: [What you need unlocked]
- **Connections**: [Intros, relationships, or access you need]

---

## Appendix
[Data tables, detailed competitive analysis, or supporting research — referenced from main slides but not presented unless asked.]
```
