# Outreach Templates by Industry Vertical

These templates are starting points. Every opener must be customized to the specific named contact before use. Replace all `[BRACKETS]` with researched specifics. A template that leaves any bracket unfilled should not be sent.

---

## RULE: What Makes an Opener Work

The opener must answer one question in the recipient's mind within the first two sentences: **"Why is this person reaching out to me specifically, right now?"**

A good opener references at least one of:
- Something the contact said publicly (earnings call quote, interview, conference talk)
- Something happening at their company right now (job posting, announced program, recent news)
- Their personal career background (prior company, prior initiative, shared experience)
- A regulatory or market event that is directly affecting their business today

A bad opener starts with anything about your firm. The firm is mentioned last, or not at all in the opening.

## LIFE SCIENCES — PHARMA/BIOTECH

**Context triggers to watch for:**
- New product launch in the next 12–18 months (= commercial infrastructure need)
- Phase 3 readout or NDA submission upcoming (= regulatory submission IT)
- Post-merger integration following acquisition
- Pharmacovigilance system compliance mandate
- SAP S/4HANA migration (nearly universal in large pharma)

**Subject line options:**
- `[COMPANY] commercial launch readiness — a question`
- `Re: [PRODUCT NAME] launch infrastructure`
- `Pharmacovigilance system consolidation — [COMPANY]`

**Opener template — New CIO, pharma:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

Your recent appointment as CIO at [COMPANY] caught my attention — you've navigated
the commercial launch infrastructure build at [PRIOR COMPANY] before, and [COMPANY]
has [PRODUCT] heading toward launch in [YEAR].

I lead [YOUR FIRM]'s life sciences technology practice and we've built the
commercial IT stack for [X] recent launches in the [THERAPEUTIC AREA] space —
including the CRM/Veeva configuration, gross-to-net, and HCP engagement platform
that [COMPARABLE COMPANY] used for their [COMPARABLE PRODUCT] launch.

Would a 30-minute call to share that playbook be useful as you're planning
your own program?
```

**Opener template — Post-acquisition integration:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

Congratulations on the [ACQUISITION TARGET] close — the ERP and quality system
integration challenge that comes with it is something we know well: [YOUR FIRM]
ran the TrackWise and SAP consolidation for [COMPARABLE COMPANY] after their
[COMPARABLE ACQUISITION] in [YEAR].

The window for getting that work scoped before the first combined audit is
shorter than most teams expect.

I'd welcome a conversation with you or whoever owns the integration workstream.
```

## LIFE SCIENCES — MEDICAL DEVICES / MEDTECH

**Context triggers:**
- FDA warning letter or 483 observation (= QMS/CAPA compliance urgency)
- EU MDR compliance deadline
- Post-acquisition integration (Medtech M&A is high volume)
- Manufacturing ERP modernization (SAP or Oracle)
- Connected device / IoT data platform

**Subject line options:**
- `[COMPANY] EU MDR compliance — [YOUR FIRM] perspective`
- `Post-[ACQUISITION] QMS integration — quick question`
- `FDA 483 response technology — [COMPANY]`

**Opener template — FDA observation follow-up:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

[COMPANY]'s recent FDA inspection and the resulting 483 observations on
[SPECIFIC AREA if public] put quality system modernization at the top of
a lot of MedTech CIO agendas right now.

[YOUR FIRM] has implemented corrective CAPA and document control platforms
for [X] device manufacturers facing similar findings — including a [COMPARABLE
COMPANY] engagement that cleared three repeat observations within 18 months
of go-live.

I'm not looking to sell you anything in this conversation — I'd like to
understand whether the systems questions are in-house or if there's a
conversation worth having.
```

**Context triggers:**
- NGS platform or bioinformatics infrastructure build-out

- Post-merger data consolidation
- LDT or IVD regulatory path (FDA CLIA, ISO 15189)
- Cloud data platform for variant analysis at scale

**Subject line options:**
- `[COMPANY] clinical IT readiness — [YOUR FIRM]`
- `Bioinformatics infrastructure for [PROGRAM NAME]`

**Opener template — Clinical market entry:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

[COMPANY]'s move toward clinical diagnostic use of [PLATFORM/ASSAY] puts a
set of IT requirements on the table that most research-stage organizations
underestimate: LIMS integration, LIS connectivity, CAP/CLIA audit trails,
and patient data governance — all before the first clinical sample.

We built that stack for [COMPARABLE COMPANY] during their transition from
research to CLIA-certified lab in [YEAR], and I think there are two or three
decisions you'll hit in the next six months where that experience would be
directly useful.

Would a call to compare notes make sense?
```

**Context triggers:**
- SAP S/4HANA migration (2027 end-of-maintenance deadline is a hard forcing function)
- Smart factory / IoT platform build
- Predictive maintenance program
- Post-acquisition ERP consolidation
- Workforce skills and training technology

**Subject line options:**
- `[COMPANY] S/4HANA migration — [YOUR FIRM]`
- `Smart factory program — question for [CIO/VP OT]`

**Opener template — S/4HANA:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

With SAP's 2027 ECC end-of-maintenance deadline approximately [X] months away,
I'm reaching out to a small number of manufacturing CIOs who have not yet
publicly announced an S/4HANA program.

[YOUR FIRM] has completed [X] S/4 migrations in discrete and process manufacturing
in the last 24 months. The honest takeaway: companies that start the business
case and landscape assessment in [CURRENT YEAR] have a realistic path to go-live
before the deadline. Companies that start in [NEXT YEAR] are in a difficult position.

I'm not trying to create urgency artificially — the deadline creates it. Is this
a conversation worth having in the next few weeks?
```

## FEDERAL / GOVERNMENT

**Context triggers:**
- IT modernization plan (FITARA mandate, Technology Modernization Fund)
- Zero Trust Architecture implementation (OMB M-22-09 deadline)
- Cloud migration (cloud-first policy)
- CDM (Continuous Diagnostics and Mitigation) program
- Active RFP or recompete on SAM.gov
- OIG or GAO finding requiring remediation

**Subject line options:**
- `[AGENCY] IT modernization — [YOUR FIRM] capabilities`
- `Re: [RFP NUMBER] — [YOUR FIRM] teaming interest`
- `Zero Trust implementation — [AGENCY]`

**Opener template — IT modernization:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

[AGENCY]'s IT Modernization Plan identified [specific initiative or gap from
the published plan] as a priority for FY[YEAR]. [YOUR FIRM] holds [contract
vehicle: e.g., CIO-SP4, SEWP, GSA MAS] and has active delivery experience
with [comparable agency] on [similar initiative].

I wanted to reach out before any formal solicitation period to understand
whether [AGENCY] is open to an industry day conversation or a capabilities
briefing on [specific technology area].

[YOUR FIRM]'s [relevant clearance level or agency experience] may be relevant
to your evaluation criteria.
```

**Opener template — Recompete:**
```
[FIRST NAME],

The [CONTRACT NAME / VEHICLE] recompete window is approaching — SAM.gov
shows a [date or quarter] expiration on the current award.

[YOUR FIRM] has been tracking [AGENCY]'s [program area] and believes we
can offer a meaningfully different approach on [specific dimension: cost,
transition risk, or technical architecture].

We are not yet in contact with the program office. Is a capabilities briefing
with [CO NAME / PM NAME] the right first step, or is there a different
engagement path you'd recommend?
```

## UNIVERSAL PITFALLS — AVOID IN ALL VERTICALS

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "I wanted to introduce [YOUR FIRM]..." | Makes it about you, not them. They delete it. |
| "We help companies like yours..." | Signals you haven't done your homework. |
| "I was hoping to connect..." | Passive. No hook. No reason to respond. |
| "Would you have 15 minutes for a call?" | Premature. The opener hasn't earned the call yet. |
| "As a leader in [your industry]..." | Flattery. Everyone sees through it. |
| Generic subject lines: "Quick question," "Following up" | Never opened. |
| Attaching a capabilities deck to the first email | Signals you are going to pitch, not have a conversation. |
| Mentioning a competitor by name in the opener | Immediately creates defensiveness. |
