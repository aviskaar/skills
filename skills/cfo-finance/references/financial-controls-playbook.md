# Financial Controls Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook defines the internal controls framework for all finance operations. Every control is designed to prevent fraud, ensure accuracy, and maintain regulatory compliance.

## 2. Month-End Close Checklist

### Week 1 (Days 1–7)
- [ ] Lock revenue: all invoices for the period issued and posted
- [ ] Confirm deferred revenue schedule is updated for all active contracts
- [ ] Clear all AP holds — every bill matched to a PO or approved
- [ ] Payroll accrual posted for mid-month if applicable

### Week 2 (Days 8–14)
- [ ] Bank statements received and reconciliation started
- [ ] Credit card statements reconciled — all charges coded and approved
- [ ] Intercompany eliminations (if applicable) completed
- [ ] Tax provisions calculated and posted

### Week 3 (Days 15–20)
- [ ] Trial balance reviewed — all accounts tied out
- [ ] AR aging reviewed; collection notes updated on all overdue accounts
- [ ] Fixed asset depreciation run for the period
- [ ] Prepaid expense amortization schedule updated

### Final Close (Days 21–25)
- [ ] Financial statements drafted (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
- [ ] Variance analysis completed (budget vs. actual, prior period comparison)
- [ ] Management commentary written for all variances > 10% or > $10K
- [ ] CFO sign-off obtained
- [ ] Board package distributed

## 3. Vendor Management Controls

### Vendor Onboarding Requirements
Before any vendor can receive payment:
1. W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (international) on file
2. Banking details verified via callbacks (never accept banking changes by email alone)
3. Vendor portal or email domain verified (check for typosquatting)
4. Contract or MSA signed with rate schedule
5. Approval from VP Finance or CFO

### Vendor Payment Controls
- All payments batch-processed on fixed days (e.g., every Tuesday and Friday)
- Dual authorization required for all payments > $10,000
- ACH/wire details can only be changed by the Controller or CFO
- No rush payments without VP Finance approval

## 5. Revenue Recognition Rules (ASC 606 / IFRS 15)

Follow the five-step model:

1. **Identify the contract** — signed agreement with payment terms
2. **Identify performance obligations** — what distinct goods/services are promised
3. **Determine transaction price** — fixed fee, variable, or milestone-based
4. **Allocate transaction price** — if multiple obligations, allocate to each based on standalone selling price
5. **Recognize revenue when/as obligations are satisfied**

### Common Revenue Scenarios

| Revenue Type | Recognition Timing |
|---|---|
| SaaS subscription | Ratably over the subscription period |
| Implementation/onboarding | As services are delivered (% completion or milestone) |
| Professional services (T&M) | As hours are logged and accepted |
| License (perpetual) | At point of delivery of license |
| Support & maintenance | Ratably over the support period |
| Variable / usage-based | As usage occurs and is measurable |

**Deferred Revenue Rule**: Cash received before services delivered → record as Deferred Revenue liability → release to Revenue as services are performed.

## 6. Audit Preparation Checklist

### Documents to Maintain at All Times
- Bank statements (7 years)
- Signed contracts with customers and vendors (7 years post-expiry)
- Payroll records including payslips (7 years)
- Tax returns and workpapers (7 years)
- Board resolutions authorizing significant transactions
- Cap table history with all equity grants documented
- Fixed asset register with purchase invoices

### Annual Audit Readiness
- Auditor receives trial balance and schedules by Day 10 post year-end
- All material journal entries have supporting documentation attached
- Related party transactions documented and disclosed
- Subsequent events reviewed through audit completion date
