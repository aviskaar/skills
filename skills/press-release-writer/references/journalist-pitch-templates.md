# Journalist Pitch Templates

Pitch emails are where most PR efforts fail. Journalists receive 200–500 pitches per week. These templates are built around the principle that a journalist's job is to find stories for their readers — not to promote your company. Every pitch must serve that goal first.

**Rule before using any template**: Read the journalist's last 3 articles in your category. The first sentence of your pitch must reference something specific from one of those articles. No reference = no read.

---

## Template 1 — Exclusive Offer (Tier-1 Journalist)

**When to use**: Major funding rounds, high-profile product launches, significant data releases. One journalist, one exclusive. Never split this.

**Email subject line options** (pick one):
- `Exclusive: [Company] raising $[X]M — embargo until [date]`
- `Exclusive + embargo: [Specific data finding] — [Company] study of [N] [subjects]`
- `Embargo for [date]: [Company] and [Known Brand] partnership announcement`

**Body:**
```
Hi [First name],

Your piece on [specific article title or topic] last [month/week] is exactly the lens through which I think you'd find this story interesting.

[Company] is [announcing / closing / launching] [what] on [date at time timezone].
The angle I think fits your beat: [one sentence connecting the story to what they cover,
not to what you want to promote].

I can offer you the exclusive — meaning you'd be the only reporter with the story before the wire.
Here's what I can give you:

- Full press release + supporting data (attaching now / sending upon confirmation)
- [20-minute briefing / 30-minute call] with [CEO name] or [relevant exec]
- [Optional: access to a customer who can speak to it / the full dataset]

Embargo: [Date, Time, Timezone]. You'd publish simultaneously with the wire.

Interested? Let me know by [deadline — 48 hours before the embargo date] and I'll confirm the exclusive.

[Your name]
[Title]
[Direct phone]
[Company]
```

**Follow-up (send once, 48 hours before deadline if no response):**
```
Hi [First name],

Following up on the exclusive below — making a final decision on [date] on who gets this story.

Happy to answer any questions or adjust the briefing format if easier.

[Your name]
```

## Template 2 — Embargo Briefing (Tier-2 Journalist)

**When to use**: After exclusive is placed (or for announcements without exclusive strategy). Offer pre-wire briefing to 5–10 journalists simultaneously but personalize each email.

**Email subject line options**:
- `[Embargo: Date] [Company] + [Partner/Event] — [One-sentence summary]`
- `Briefing: [Company]'s [announcement] — under embargo until [date]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [First name],

I read your coverage of [specific article — 2–4 words is fine]. This announcement is a direct extension of that story.

[Company] is [announcing/launching/publishing] [what] on [date].
Brief summary:
- [Bullet 1: the core news in one sentence]
- [Bullet 2: the proof point — number, named logo, or data finding]
- [Bullet 3: why this matters to their readers specifically]

Under embargo until [date, time, timezone]. Happy to offer a [15-minute briefing /
supporting data / executive access] if that would be useful for your coverage.

[Your name]
[Title]
[Direct phone]
```

**If they respond with interest:**
Send: full press release, supporting data, and suggested expert quotes.
Offer: a calendar link for a briefing call within 24 hours.

## Template 3 — Post-Wire Pitch (No Prior Relationship)

**When to use**: After wire drops and you're pitching journalists who didn't receive a pre-brief.

**Email subject line options**:
- `[Company]'s [announcement]: story angle for [publication name]`
- `For [beat]: [One surprising fact from the press release]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [First name],

[Company] published a press release this morning on [what].
The angle I think fits [publication name]: [one sentence on the specific story,
connecting it to the journalist's beat and their readers — not to your product].

The most interesting data point: [the most counterintuitive finding or the
biggest number from the release].

Happy to connect you with [CEO / customer / data] if useful for a story.
Full release here: [URL]

[Your name]
[Title]
[Direct phone]
```

## Template 4 — Industry Analyst Briefing Request

**When to use**: Pre-announcement briefings to Gartner, Forrester, IDC, or other analysts who cover your category.

**When to send**: 7–14 days before the public announcement.

**Email subject:**
`Briefing request: [Company] — [Category] announcement, [Month Year]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Analyst name],

We're briefing analysts ahead of a [product launch / partnership / funding announcement]
that directly affects the [category name] market you cover.

Specific topic: [One sentence on what you're announcing and why it's relevant to their coverage area]

What we'd like to share:
- [Announcement overview and market context]
- [Internal data we haven't published publicly]
- [Product roadmap elements relevant to their Magic Quadrant / Wave / report]

We're not asking for a quote or an endorsement — we want your perspective on how this fits
the market landscape you track. Off the record is fine.

Available: [3 specific time slots in their timezone]
Duration: 30 minutes

[Your name]
[Title]
[Company]
```

## Template 5 — Podcast or Newsletter Pitch

**When to use**: For founders, executives, or subject-matter experts pitching guest appearances or story inclusions in industry newsletters.

**Email subject options**:
- `Guest pitch for [Podcast Name]: [Specific topic tied to recent episode]`
- `Story for [Newsletter]: [Counterintuitive finding or angle]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

I listened to [specific episode or read specific newsletter issue] — your take on
[specific point they made] is exactly the context for why I'm reaching out.

[Name], [Title] at [Company], has a take that [challenges / extends / contradicts]
that point with [specific data or experience].

Specifically: [2–3 sentences on what the guest would contribute that's
genuinely useful to the audience — not a product pitch].

[Name]'s background in short: [2 sentences — most impressive credential, most
relevant experience, one contrarian position they can defend].

Happy to send a full speaker bio, talking points, or a sample question list.

[Your name]
[Title]
[Email / Phone]
```

## Template 6 — Data Exclusive (Research Release)

**When to use**: Publishing original research or a data report. Journalists love exclusive data — especially if it's surprising.

**Email subject:**
`Exclusive data: [Surprising finding] — [Company] study of [N] [subjects]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [First name],

I have data I think you'll want before we publish it publicly.

[Company] surveyed/analyzed [N] [subjects] between [date range] on [topic].
The finding that surprised us most: [the most counterintuitive data point].

Secondary findings:
- [Finding 2 with number]
- [Finding 3 with number]

This directly connects to your recent coverage of [specific article they wrote].

I can offer you:
- The full dataset (raw CSV / aggregated tables)
- An embargo until [date] to write the story first
- Access to [relevant expert at company / customer / outside expert] for comment

Let me know if this fits something you're working on.

[Your name]
[Title]
[Direct phone]
```

## Pitch Rules Summary

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| 150 words maximum for initial pitch | Journalists delete long pitches unread. If you can't summarize in 150 words, the story isn't clear yet. |
| Personalize the first sentence for every journalist | Mass-blast pitches are identifiable and go straight to delete |
| Send Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM in their timezone | Highest open and response rates; avoids Monday backlog and Friday checkout |
| Follow up exactly once, with a new fact | Second follow-up with no new information signals desperation; new data gives them a reason to re-engage |
| Never follow up more than twice | Respect journalists' time. Three+ follow-ups damages the relationship permanently. |
| Use subject lines with numbers, names, or embargo flags | These outperform vague subject lines 3:1 in response rate |
| Never pitch and CC a competitor journalist | If journalists see others were pitched simultaneously, the exclusivity of the story collapses |
| Never pitch without knowing the journalist's beat | A security reporter pitched a fintech story will remember the sender as one who wastes their time |

## Journalist Research Checklist

Before pitching any journalist, complete this in under 10 minutes:

- [ ] Read their last 5 articles (note: beat focus, preferred story type, publication audience)
- [ ] Check their Twitter/X for what they're currently interested in
- [ ] Identify their preferred pitch format (some journalists publicly state preferences in their bio)
- [ ] Confirm they are still at the publication (bylines go stale — always verify on the publication's staff page)
- [ ] Find their direct email (use the publication's standard format: firstname@publication.com or firstname.lastname@publication.com — never use contact@ or press@)
- [ ] Note any recent interactions they had with competitors (they may be covering a competitive story)

## Building Long-Term Journalist Relationships

One placement is not a relationship. A relationship is built over 12–18 months through:

1. **Sharing tips that have nothing to do with your company** — If you see a story that fits their beat, email them with no ask. Do this 3–4× per year.
2. **Connecting them to expert sources** — When they tweet asking for experts to interview, make introductions. Expect nothing in return.
3. **Responding to their requests for comment** — When they're writing a story that touches your space and reach out, always respond within 2 hours.
4. **Acknowledging good coverage** — A short "your piece on X was the best I've read on that topic" email (no ask attached) builds goodwill.
5. **Never pitching off-beat** — If a journalist covers SaaS enterprise software and you're launching a consumer app, do not pitch them. Respecting their beat is respecting their time.

Track every journalist relationship in a simple CRM or spreadsheet:
- Date of last interaction
- What you last sent or discussed
- Whether they have covered you before
- Their current beat and publication
- Preferred contact method
