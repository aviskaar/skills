# Headline Formulas for Press Releases

40 proven headline structures organized by announcement type. Each formula is tested against the criteria: specificity, urgency, clarity, and journalist usability.

**Usage rule**: Fill in every bracket with a specific fact. If you cannot fill a bracket without being vague, choose a different formula.

---

## Funding Round Headlines

1. `[Company] Raises $[X]M to [Solve Problem] as [Market Trend] Hits Inflection Point`
   - Example: *Vendor raises $50M to eliminate invoice fraud as AP teams face 300% volume surge*

2. `With $[X]M in New Funding, [Company] Aims to [Bold Market Claim]`
   - Example: *With $30M in new funding, Flowable aims to replace the enterprise ERP for mid-market ops teams*

3. `[Lead Investor]'s $[X]M Bet on [Company] Is a Bet Against [Status Quo Practice]`
   - Example: *Sequoia's $75M bet on FieldOps is a bet against the corporate travel expense report*

4. `[Company] Closes $[X]M to Expand [Product/Feature] After [Traction Proof Point]`
   - Example: *Dataloop closes $28M to expand MLOps platform after tripling enterprise customers in 12 months*

5. `Backed by $[X]M, [Company] Takes Aim at [Specific $XB Market]`
   - Example: *Backed by $45M, Remediant takes aim at the $12B privileged access management market*

6. `[Company] Secures $[X]M, Betting [Industry] Will [Bold Prediction]`
   - Example: *Workstream secures $60M, betting restaurant HR will move fully AI-native by 2026*

## Product Launch Headlines

7. `[Company] Launches [Product] That [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe]`
   - Example: *Ramp launches AP automation that closes the books in 2 hours instead of 2 days*

8. `New [Product Name] From [Company]: [Specific Claim] Without [The Tradeoff]`
   - Example: *New Comply360 from Vanta: SOC 2 in 6 weeks without a dedicated compliance team*

9. `[Company]'s [Product Name] Replaces [Status Quo Tool] for [ICP]`
   - Example: *Notion's AI editor replaces the first draft for knowledge workers*

10. `[Company] Introduces [Product]: The First [Category Claim] Built for [Specific Persona]`
    - Example: *Pulley introduces Carta Alternative: the first cap table platform built for employee ownership transparency*

11. `[Company] Ships [Product] — [One-Line Differentiator]`
    - Example: *Linear ships AI planning — Jira complexity, minus the Jira*

12. `[Product Name] Arrives: [Company] Targets $[X]B [Market] With [Specific Approach]`
    - Example: *Glean Arrives: Anthropic spinout targets $8B enterprise search market with RAG-first approach*

13. `[Company] Solves [Specific Problem] With [Product Name] — Available Now for [ICP]`
    - Example: *Ironclad solves contract redlining bottlenecks with NegotiateAI — available now for in-house legal teams*

## Partnership Headlines

14. `[Company A] and [Company B] Join Forces to [Specific Outcome] for [Customer Segment]`
    - Example: *Stripe and Xero join forces to automate reconciliation for 3.7 million small businesses*

15. `[Company A]'s Integration With [Company B] Cuts [Metric] by [X%] for Joint Customers`
    - Example: *HubSpot's integration with Gong cuts rep prep time by 40% for joint customers*

16. `[Company A] + [Company B]: [What the Combination Enables] for [Persona]`
    - Example: *Salesforce + Slack: one-click deal room creation for enterprise sales teams*

17. `Through New Partnership, [Company A] Brings [Capability] to [Company B]'s [N]M Users`
    - Example: *Through new partnership, Plaid brings real-time income verification to TransUnion's 200M credit files*

18. `[Company A] and [Company B] Eliminate the [Pain Point] That Has Plagued [Industry] for [Timeframe]`
    - Example: *Workday and ServiceNow eliminate the employee data gap that has plagued enterprise IT for a decade*

## Executive Hire Headlines

19. `[Company] Appoints [Name] as [Title] to Lead [Strategic Initiative]`
    - Example: *Brex appoints Camilla Matias as Chief Revenue Officer to lead enterprise expansion*

20. `Former [Previous Title] at [Notable Company] Joins [Company] to [Specific Goal]`
    - Example: *Former VP Engineering at Stripe joins Mercury to build the developer banking platform*

21. `[Company] Brings on [Name] — Who [Specific Past Achievement] — as [Title]`
    - Example: *Temporal brings on James Cowling — who scaled Dropbox storage to 1B files/day — as VP Infrastructure*

22. `[Name], Who [Built/Scaled/Led] [Notable Achievement], Named [Title] at [Company]`
    - Example: *Sarah Chen, who grew Twilio's developer community from 500K to 10M, named CMO at Courier*

## Customer Win / Milestone Headlines

23. `[Company] Hits [Milestone] — [Timeframe Comparison That Shows Velocity]`
    - Example: *Figma hits 4M users — faster than Slack reached the same milestone from launch*

24. `[X]% of [Reference Group] Now Use [Company] — Up From [Y]% [Timeframe] Ago`
    - Example: *38% of Fortune 500 legal teams now use ContractPodAi — up from 12% two years ago*

25. `[Company] Surpasses [Metric], Adding [N] [Customers/Users/Locations] in [Timeframe]`
    - Example: *Rho surpasses $2B in cards processed, adding 1,400 mid-market finance teams in Q3 alone*

26. `How [Company] Grew [Metric] [X]× in [Timeframe] — And What Drove It`
    - Example: *How Runway grew ARR 6× in 18 months — and what drove it*

27. `[Company]'s Customer Base Doubled in [Timeframe]. Here's Why [Trend] Is the Cause.`
    - Example: *Secureframe's customer base doubled in 8 months. Here's why the SOC 2 audit surge is the cause.*

## Research / Data Release Headlines

28. `New Data: [Surprising Fact] — [Company]'s Study of [N] [Subjects] Reveals`
    - Example: *New Data: 72% of enterprise procurement deals stall over vendor security reviews — Vendr's study of 5,000 SaaS purchases reveals*

29. `[X]% of [Audience] [Counterintuitive Behavior], [Company] Research Finds`
    - Example: *61% of CFOs plan to cut headcount before cutting software spend, Pigment research finds*

30. `The [Annual/Quarterly] [Industry] Report: [Key Finding That Challenges Conventional Wisdom]`
    - Example: *The 2025 Engineering Velocity Report: teams with fewer meetings ship 2.3× faster*

31. `[Company]'s Benchmark Study: [Category Leaders] [Achieve Outcome] [X]× Faster Than [Laggards]`
    - Example: *Lattice's Benchmark Study: top-quartile people teams resolve performance issues 4× faster than bottom-quartile*

32. `[Industry] Is [Broken / Changing / At Risk]. [N] Data Points Show Why.`
    - Example: *Sales onboarding is broken. 12 data points show why new reps take 9 months to reach quota.*

## Contrarian / Thought-Leadership Headlines

33. `[Company] Bets [Bold Claim] — Here's the Data Behind It`
    - Example: *Deel bets remote-first won't reverse — here's the data behind it*

34. `Everyone Is [Common Practice]. [Company] Is Doing the Opposite.`
    - Example: *Everyone is adding AI to their product. Basecamp is doing the opposite.*

35. `Why [Company] Chose [Unconventional Decision] — And What It Found`
    - Example: *Why Notion chose to sunset its API-only tier — and what it found when it did*

36. `[Company] Proves That [Counterintuitive Claim] Is Better for [Metric]`
    - Example: *Calendly proves that removing sales reps from the discovery call is better for close rates*

## Crisis / Turnaround / Response Headlines

37. `[Company] Addresses [Issue], Announces [Specific Corrective Action]`
    - Example: *Superhuman addresses data privacy concerns, announces end-to-end encryption for all tiers*

38. `After [Event], [Company] Is Changing [Specific Policy / Practice / Product]`
    - Example: *After Q3 outage, PagerDuty is changing its incident response SLA — and publishing the new metrics*

39. `[Company] Responds to [Issue] With [Concrete Action] — [Timeframe] Commitment`
    - Example: *Cloudflare responds to BGP routing incident with public post-mortem and 72-hour remediation commitment*

---

## Award / Recognition Headlines

40. `[Company] Named [Award] — What [Industry] Recognition Signals About [Trend]`
    - Example: *Figma named Gartner Magic Quadrant leader — what design tool category consolidation signals about the no-code market*

**Note on award headlines**: Never let the award be the story. The award is evidence. The story is what the award signals about the market, the category, or the buyer's decision-making. A journalist covers market trends, not trophy cases.

---

## Headline Anti-Patterns — Never Use These

| Weak Formula | Why It Fails | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| `[Company] Announces [Product]` | "Announces" is press release jargon, not news | Use: launches, ships, closes, publishes, reveals |
| `[Company] Is Pleased to Announce` | Sounds like a wedding invitation | Delete the phrase; start with the news |
| `[Company]: A Leader in [Category]` | Self-declared leadership is not news | Cite a third-party ranking or a specific market position |
| `[Company] Unveils Revolutionary [Product]` | "Revolutionary" is unverifiable | Replace with the specific thing that is different |
| `[Company] and [Partner] Enter Into Strategic Partnership` | "Strategic" is filler | State what the partnership does for customers |
| `[Company] Achieves Significant Milestone` | "Significant" is relative and vague | State the actual milestone with a number |
